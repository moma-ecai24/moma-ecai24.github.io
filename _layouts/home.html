I am a PhD student at the Autonomous Agents and Distributed Intelligence Lab, part of the Collaborative Robotics and Intelligent Systems (CoRIS) Institute at Oregon State University, where I work on learning and coordination in multi-robot systems. I am advised by Dr. Kagan Tumer.

My research is at the intersection of reinforcement learning, evolutionary robotics and game theory. I am interested in developing methods that enable asymmetric robots (robots with diverse capabilities and objectives) to cooperate and work together as teams with a variety of partners. Discovering the relationships between robots and their influence on the team dynamics is crucial for learning adaptable behaviors that can generalize across a variety of tasks and team compositions. For cultivating such relationships, my work investigates Quality-Diversity and Coevolutionary reinforcement learning algorithms which encourage novel behaviors and teamwork as a key component of the learning process.
